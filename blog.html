Panel Discussion: Opportunities and Considerations for Interactive Community Gardens

date: monday 11dec

time: 12:30 - 1:30 (lunch from 12:15)

venue: GPS 78-209, University of Queensland

[Dr Jacki Liddle] [Dr Peter Lyle] [Dr Theresa Scott]

As many cities, including Brisbane, develop strategies and take actions to become "Smart Cities", their motivations naturally focus on economic development and benefits. One of the objectives of the Smart City vision is to improve the quality of life of the residents of that city. Certainly, advancing economic development will do this ... at least for some of those residents.

Cities such as Brisbane have recognised the need to "empower and enable" (see the Digital Brisbane 2.0 strategy) residents to access and utilise the services made available through the infrastructure that is the backbone of any Smart City. But in reality, the only people who are able to do this are those who are not just "tech savvy" but those who are able to code and access that infrastructure: creating and appropriating devices, navigating API's and managing cloud services.

There is a growing movement that seeks to move away from the corporate-focused and controlled Smart-City. One that realises "cities are about people, and the introduction of smart city technology should be informed by the user experience and led by people" (Marcus Foth, 2017).

In this project we are starting small: exploring how technology could be added to a community garden to improve the experience for the people who use or might want to use that garden. This panel discussion brings together three researchers from different disciplines who have researched people and community gardens. Together with this panel we will explore and discuss: the work that each of these researchers have done in this domain; the people who use these spaces, why and how they want to use them; things that we need to consider as we create an interactive garden space; and, opportunities that we should investigate further.

This panel discussion will relevant to those interested in: Interaction Design, Human-centred Design, HCI, Interactive Spaces, UX, Community Gardens, Gardens and Wellness.
