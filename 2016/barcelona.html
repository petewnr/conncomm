Barcelona

How the City Defines Itself

Barcelona calssifies itself as a city of the people, it seeks to improve citizens welfare and quality of life as well as to foster economic progress.

The Barcelona City Council run the Barcelona Smart City Project, and it has created a framework in order to organise itself. The frame work sets out its priororties in a list and what each task entails.

The Framework : Key Points

The over arching plan can be seen above, citizens, economy and institution, is stated first and then expanded on below.

Some key points to be seen in the framework is the priorotization of the People and Families. The quote, or goal, of "Favourite city to live with security, equity and quality of life" gives support to their people first initiative, that the people of the city come first.

The next aspect is hidden behind the graphic, but the third is economy and knowledge centres. The desire for a smart city requires funding, and while the council is the creator and manager they do require outside assistance. This will be explored further at a later point in the disscussion.

The remaining framework utilizes the structure and resources and can be further explored below, but one interesting point is the placement of the budget in the framework, and how it could be seen as uninfluencial factor towards the smart city initiative.

Target of the City

The city takes divides its initiative into 3 stratigic areas, Internation Promotion, International Collabouration and Local Projects.

The Barcelona Smart City project promotes itself via a wide range of international expo's, conferences and shows. These include the Smart City Expo, Global Clean Energy Forum and the European Conference on e-Public Procurement.

Along with international promotion the city participates in major events such as the Meeting of the Minds, the Annual Smart Grids, Smart Cities and the Biennale Architeture : Common Ground.

The final focus area of the project, are the local projects. These are ongoing and vary in size, complexity and focus. There are over 100 projects, and some examples are listed and sicussed below.

What Makes the City Smart

The 100+ projects that have both physical and digital dimensions are what makes the city smart. High-quality urban planning and renewal projects along with historical preservation, as well as providing digital connectivity via fibreoptic, wifi and sensors. Tag cloud opinions allow the people to be more involved and engaged during events by creating, sending and sharing personal content via mobile. People can express their ideas and know what is happening in real time throughout the city.

SCOPE

The Barcelona City Council gathers the data and then works with people and business who request acess to the information gathered. They use smart city frameworks such as CityOS , CityScapeSDK and OpenDataBCN.

Future Plan

The 2020 vision is for barcelona to become a global reference model for the development of an urban innovation ecosystem.

Who helped create it?

The city council created and manages the project, and has agreements with Sentilo (Smart cities framework), City OS (Smart cities framework) and OpenDataBCN (Barcellona city hall database). As well as BIM/sa (Bank of Barcelona), Barcelona Municiple Services Company (company of the barcelona city council) and TMB. TMB is the main public transport operator in Catalonia (section of spain) and a benchmark public mobility company in Europe and the world. BCN ecological - BCNecologia is a public consortium dedicated to rethink cities in key of sustainability. Barcelona uses the city plan as a framework and along with a great range of sensors collects data for people and business to use, with the aim of building a smart city together.
