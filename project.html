the project

How should smart cities be designed for people?

Through a community garden, we want to explore a different vision for a smart city.

Many cities around the world are now declaring themselves as "Smart Cities". On one definition:

A smart city is an urban area that uses different types of electronic data collection sensors to supply information used to manage assets and resources efficiently. This includes data collected from citizens, devices, and assets that is processed and analyzed to monitor and manage traffic and transportation systems, power plants, water supply networks, waste management, law enforcement, information systems, schools, libraries, hospitals, and other community services.

Wikipedia Smart City available here , retrieved on 23 November 2017

The Australian Government has a plan encouraging Australian cities to become smart cities and in 2016 conducted a consultation with the State and Local Governments about this plan. Brisbane has been involved with these consultations. Brisbane’s stated vision is consistent with what is happening else where in the world, where smart cities are being driven by three motivations:

  • economics - attracting and retaining businesses and industry seen as being important
  • population demographics - attracting people with the relevant skills to support businesses and industry
  • sustainability - making the cities more efficient and environmentally friendly

However there is another view of a smart city. This view increases the focus on the people and citizens who live in those smart cities, where “urban citizens” are engaged and empowered to act to address problems that they think are important to improve their lives.

To make this happen the technology must be understandable and usable, the data that is collected must be accessible, and real value or benefit must be delivered to those citizen.

The focus of this project is to explore how this alternative view of a smart city can be made a reality through technology.
